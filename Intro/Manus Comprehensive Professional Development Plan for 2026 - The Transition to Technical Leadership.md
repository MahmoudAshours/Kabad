### Specific Advice for Your Transition

Based on the highly structured nature of your 2025 plan, here are three pieces of advice to ensure a successful 2026:

1. **Shift from Consumption to Creation:** Your 2025 plan was heavily weighted toward consuming content (articles, videos, books). In 2026, you must prioritize **output and delivery**. For every hour spent reading, spend three hours applying that knowledge to a high-impact project, writing a design document, or mentoring a colleague.
2. **Embrace Ambiguity and Delegation:** Senior and Staff roles involve solving problems that are ill-defined and often span organizational boundaries. You must become comfortable with **starting without a clear answer** and **delegating** implementation details. Your job is to define the _what_ and _why_, not always the _how_.
3. **Replace LeetCode with System Deep Dives:** Your daily LeetCode habit served its purpose. Now, replace that time with **System Deep Dives**. Choose a critical production system (internal or external, like a major cloud service) and spend the time analyzing its architecture, failure modes, and cost structure. This is the type of problem-solving that defines the next level of your career.

By focusing on **impact, influence, and strategic ownership** in 2026, you will successfully transition from a top-tier Senior Software Engineer to a respected technical leader.
